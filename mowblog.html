Meals on Wheels Redesign

As part of my graphic design studies, I was tasked to redesign the brand of a non-profit organization I was invested in. Having volunteered for Meals on Wheels a few times myself, I took on the challenge to develop a logo involving a natural blend of type and imagery.

Redesign the Redesign

Before landing on my current iteration, I had already tried my hand at it in a previous course. I pulled many ideas to stay consistent in my conceptualization. The "M", and inversely "W", motif persists, but with more unity and a better sense of rhythm. I also lost the human side of the brand's image by using faceless globs exchanging a similarly mysterious package. Look at the fellows on the right, do they not look happy to be human?

Graphic Standard Manual

The graphic standard manual is a collection of best practices and acts as a guide to designers working with client-controlled assets. As part of my redesign, I also designed a small booklet going into the intricacies of using my newly beloved logo.
